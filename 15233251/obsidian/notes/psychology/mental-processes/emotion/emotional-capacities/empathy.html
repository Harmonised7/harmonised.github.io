Empathy

Seeking to Understand/Experience how another person is truly Feeling, by Analyzing/Listening to their Situation, so that we could have a better image of how and where from these Feelings arose from, to be able to develop a hopefully accurate sense of how the other person is Feeling, developing a Connection through shared Feelings.

Empathy without actually Feeling and instead Faking the Feelings is called Reflection.

Empathy is an essential Social Skill, and is especially powerful in Validation.

Notes

  • Empathy is different from Sympathy by introduction of seeking genuine Connection.
    • Sympathy: "I'm sorry you're Sad."
      • "This inconveniences me, and I don't like that."
    • Empathy: "Wow, this is Sad."
      • "I Processed this, and I am also Sad about it/think it is Sad."
  • Empathy is always Important in any Relationship, at any Time/Circumstance.
    • Empathy is what Allows us to Receive Feedback on the other person's Feelings, and Allows us to fairly Accurately Determine what is Appropriate and what is not in the given Situation.
      • When we Observe/Recognize how someone is Feeling, we can then match their Energy, and Determine what is the Appropriate way to Behave/Respond.
        • If the Person is Experiencing:
          • Negative Feelings: Sad/Exhausted/Overwhelmed/Hurting/Grieving/etc...
            • Lower your Energy and Give Them Space. No Pushing. The last thing they need is being given another Reason to Feel worse.
          • Positive Feelings: Happy/Excited/Playful/Giddy/etc...
            • Only if you have the Energy to spare, and want to Connect with this Person: Raise your Energy and join in their Positivity! Be Happy for them~

Empathy Tips

  • Get Curious
    • Think why this person may be Feeling this way.
    • How would you Feel if you've went through a similar Experience?
    • What would have to happen to you to Feel this way?
  • Look At Them
    • Identify that this is an Imperfect living being, a person with Fears, Desires, Hopes, Wants, and Needs that is not unlike yourself, and that they are Valid.
    • Recognize that they may be going through Pain, Grief, or Struggles otherwise that you may not be Aware of, and how Difficult they may be on the person, likely affecting their Behavior, and if you were in a similar Situation - you would likely Respond and Behave very Similarly.
      • And that is not something to Judge the person for, as you have your own Struggles, and know how Difficult they can be, and are - regardless of who Understands them or not.
  • Imagine Them As A Child
    • Imagine them as a small child, someone who has not yet had the time nor Experience to Develop a Sense for what Feelings are, what they mean, and how to Process them.
      • This helps prevent Judgment, and see the person more clearly.
  • Learn To Identify Your Own Emotions
    • Pay Attention to how and what you Feel yourself throughout the day, and why.
      • Perhaps leave a daily reminder.
  • Quit Judging Your Own Emotions
    • When Identifying your own Feelings and Emotions - pay Attention to if you are Suppressing, Avoiding, or Accepting your own Feelings and Emotions.
  • Dedicate your full Attention to the person (read Respect).
