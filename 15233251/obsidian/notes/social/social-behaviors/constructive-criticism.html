Constructive Criticism

Honest Criticism, Deliberately Designed as Constructive Feedback.

  • Constructive Criticism has Positive intent of Providing Honest, Constructive Feedback, with only the Intent separating it from a Deliberate Insult.
  • Constructive Criticism is a Skill.
    • A poor Attempt at Constructively Criticizing due to lack of Skill or otherwise will Result in Perception as an Insult.
  • Constructive Criticism Requires Wisdom
    • Regardless of your Skill in Constructive Criticism: using it Appropriately is essential.
      • Whether due to poor History with the person, or lack of of Receptiveness: your Attempt at Constructive Criticism may fail.

Tips

  • Avoid Labeling and Stating.
    • Emphasize that this is your Opinion rather than a Fact.
    • Imagine you drew a picture
      • Insultingly Criticizing
        • "That tree sucks."
        • "The sky is unrealistic."
        • "The face is all messed up."
          • Notice how Insults are Statements, Suggesting that they are Final by use of a Label, Invalidating Opinions/Interpretations of others.
      • Constructively Criticizing
        • "I think that tree looks off."
        • "In my opinion, the sky's hue doesn't match the rest of the scenery."
        • "It seems to me that there may be something off with the face."
          • Notice how Criticisms are Personal, Suggesting that they are not Final and that they are Personal Opinions, Validating Opinions/Interpretations of others.
