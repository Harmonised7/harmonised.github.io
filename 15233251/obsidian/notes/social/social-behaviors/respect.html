Respect Tips

One of the most Sincere forms of Respect is truly hearing what the other person has to say (Empathy).

Receiving Respect makes us Feel Recognized, Important (A Priority) and Feeling Valued by the person showing the Respect.

Give the person your full Attention.

  • Whatever you may be Focused on in the moment - prioritize your Attention to the person (provided you want to give the person your Respect).
    • Do not Fool yourself that you can Successfully Fool the other person into Believing that you are giving your full Attention while you are not.
      • We as Social creatures: pick up on Social things automatically. It's in our Nature.
        • If something feels off - we will have a Sense for it, regardless if Consciously, or Subconsciously.
          • As an example:
            • Have you ever had a Conversation with someone who Accepted your Request to have one, yet it Feels like something else is on their mind? Like they aren't Being Present in the Conversation?
            • Like your Conversation is one-sided, or is simply not the primary Focus, not the primary Concern, like there is something more Important to the person in the moment?
            • They may be able to Repeat, perhaps even Rephrase everything you said Successfully, yet it still Feels Off? Like there is a Disconnect between you two?
            • You're not Crazy. And it wasn't Rude or Demanding or Judgmental of you to Observe this.
            • You simply Paid Attention To your Social Senses, that are telling you something Feels Off. Good Job!
            • This person Attempted to Fool your Social Senses, whether Consciously or Unconsciously. They may not have done it out of Malice specifically. However, it was still Inconsiderate and Rude of them.
              • It really doesn't Feel Good, to know that the other person is Prioritizing you and the Activity you were both meant to be Sharing below whatever is on their mind instead, does it?
                • Significantly worse than if the person never Accepted the Conversation in the first place, because then: at least your Emotional Energy and Time would not have been Wasted.
            • Don't be like them.
  • Fully pause what you're doing.
  • Help them Feel Heard, show them they're Important to you.
  • Make them a Priority over your now-past Activity.
    • Pause your video.
    • Take out your Earbuds.
    • Take off your Headphones.
    • Take out your Earplugs.
      • Note that for the 3 items above there may be a Concern of Damaging your Hearing in a loud Environment, or Introduction of a Source of Irritation for your Sensitive Hearing, in which case you should absolutely Prioritize your Safety and Comfort. Put On Your Oxygen Mask First.
    • Put away your Book.
    • Put away your Phone.
      • Put it out of sight.
      • Phones are your gateway to the rest of the world. Studies have shown that having your Phone out in sight, even if the screen is off is Subconsciously Worrying of being less Important in the Conversation. It is Disconnecting.
    • Look at the person.
      • If you're Uncomfortable with that for any reason - look away from your previous activity. From anything that would indicate your Attention is elsewhere.
        • Clock
        • Window
        • Puzzle
    • Above and beyond extra special Gestures, that are So Overkill, that you simply Must Recognize and Appreciate, knowing they were done for you, even though they weren't Strictly Necessary.
      • Turn off the monitor/tv entirely.
      • Shut the laptop close, even if it's only showing a blank screen.
  • If you are currently too Busy and truly cannot Dedicate your full, undivided Attention to the person, to a point where it would Hurt you or otherwise significantly Inconvenience you in some way - whether it's a deadline, Stress, or current Inability due to exhaustion or otherwise - then don't! Instead:
    • Express this to the person. Let them know that while you cannot Provide your full Attention that they and their Troubles are still Important to you despite it.
      • "I'm sorry, this isn't a good time for me right now. This sounds Important and I would like to give you my full Attention and I am too Exhausted to do that right now."
      • "Sorry, I am in the middle of a Stressful deadline that's due in a few hours from now and I would be Distracted by it if we were to talk about this right now and that would not sit right with me. Can we talk in an hour? I want to give you my full Attention."
    • Being Honest with the person shows that you Trust them enough to be Transparent with them (as opposed to an Excuse) and Maintains your Boundaries which the person will likely Recognize and Decide on their own terms (instead of being told "My Worries are more Important than your Worries, which would do nothing but hurt the Relationship.) that your Needs and Boundaries have higher Priority than their Issues and choose to Respect them.
      • Your Confidence and Emotional Capacity to be able to Protect your Boundaries is Admirable, and worthy of Respect, perhaps even Looking Up To.
      • It is a Far more Effective tactic than:
        • Making up Excuses to "spare" their Feelings (Hurting them instead through lack of Sincerity, showing them they cannot be Trusted with the Truth and instead given Excuses.)
        • Enduring the Inconvenience and Pushing through regardless.
          • Potentially causing Resentment towards the Situation that you allowed to happen.
          • Exhausting yourself even further and showing lack of Attention to the person by being Inattentive, showing a lack of Interest - which is Disrespectful instead.
        • All of the above Hurting the Relationship, instead of Developing it.
      • Of course Urgency is absolutely a factor. If the person comes in all Panicked, in Shock, or Distressed otherwise - the appropriate Priority must be established.
