disconnection

disconnect

Is the vibe of not being on the same page. When you and the other person are doing the same activity/Are in the same conversation, but are seemingly perceiving two different experiences.

Examples

  • You are Super excited over the upcoming holiday with your best friend, you express how wonderful it's going to be, how much you cannot wait to see all the Beaches/Snow, and yet they don't seem to share your enthusiasm one bit.
    • "It's as if they don't even care..."
  • Your friend is Super interested in this stupid little car figurine, and you just cannot understand why. They push it into your face all excited and you just don't know what to do with that request, and cannot think of what to respond with, so you just stare at it and your friend...
    • "Uhhh, what the hell do they expect me to say..? It's just a stupid toy..?"
  • When you're feeling awful about something and seek validation from your family member, and they offer you a solution instead...
    • "It's like they don't even care for about my feelings... I feel even worse now, I feel misunderstood, I feel alone..."
  • When the person you're with is Incredibly worried about something you don't see the significance of, like if you let's say got a small cut that bled a little, or slipped on the ground and didn't really hurt yourself, or got sick from getting too cold, yet the other person is freaking out. You explain the situation and it's severity (or rather: lack of), and the other person seemingly only worries more. It feels disconnecting.
    • Surely the victim - which is you - knows the severity of the damage/seriousness better than the other person - and you have confirmed that there is nothing to worry about.
      • Depending on the person - you may even really appreciate their care and worry, yet the fact is that their worry is now bringing you extra stress and worry, which in case of sickness - may actually escalate the sickness, potentially worsening the severity of it, and prolonging it.
        • "I already explained my symptoms in full without sugarcoating anything and yet they're still worried, and I can tell there is nothing I can do... I wish we could be on the same page again, because I really appreciate their love, care, and support, to help me feel better by being here for me and being strong for me, because I surely am not strong myself right now. Instead of causing me to worry and stress about their worries and stressing about me... I don't want them to feel bad over me, it makes me feel guilty and encourages me to focus the little spare energy I have remaining that is Much better spent fighting off my sickness and helping me recover, instead of worrying about how difficult I am making it on them, causing me to expend some of my currently extra precious energy to making sure they have it easy as possible..."
