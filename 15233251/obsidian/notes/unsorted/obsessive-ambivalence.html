obsessive ambivalence

  • When judging something or someone: We immediately pick a side first: positive or negative. Especially for topics we don't understand or people we've never meeting or properly interacted with!
    • Example:
      • What is your opinion on AI?
        • You probably picked a side the moment you saw it. AI is great, or AI is terrible. But what do you actually know about AI? It's a Far too complex to label as inherently Good or Bad.
      • What is your opinion on vaccines?
        • This is one of the reasons that there are idiots fighting against vaccines. They interpreted a side based on their past knowledge of the topic, even though in reality: They know virtually nothing true about this topic. Probably only myths and propaganda.
      • Yet we as humans immediately pick a side, before we think about the topic.
        • Many end up never thinking about the topic, yet defending their belief regardless.
      • Don't be like them.
    • challenge your initial assessment with facts and logic.
      • research and study the topic to get a good understanding of the concept.
