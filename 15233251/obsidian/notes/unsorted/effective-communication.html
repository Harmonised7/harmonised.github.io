Effective Communication

Tips

  • References
    • Validate
      • Don't try to fix it!
    • Being Constructive
      • Constructive Feedback
        • Constructive Criticism
        • Constructive Complimenting
  • Connect
    • Invite them to Open Up by Providing them a Safe Space.
      • Help them Feel Heard and Feel Valued by Listening to their Concerns, Difficulties, and Struggles, and Validating the Feelings and Emotions caused by them.
    • Observe their Feelings and Emotions
      • Body Language
        • Someone may Convey Shyness, Timidness, Confidence, etc... In their Body Language.
      • Language
        • Intonation
          • "I'm Really Tired..."
          • "I absolutely Love that!"
        • Tone Of Voice
          • Someone may Convey Anger, Sadness, Tiredness, etc... In their Voice.
    • Recognize the Appropriate Behavior in the current Situation.
      • If someone is being Reluctant - you may Deduce that they are: Embarrassed, Worried about being Judged or Harmed or otherwise, or that they are Experiencing some sort of Discomfort through some other way.
        • This is Important information that Helps us Decide what is the most Appropriate way to proceed with this Conversation, such as:
          • Taking A Break
          • Giving Some Space
          • Advancing the topic
          • Respecting Boundaries
          • etc...
    • Match Their Energy
      • It is Disconnecting to have different Energy levels in a Conversation.
        • If you are Excited and Share something you are Happy about - you are most likely doing so in hopes of Receiving Validation. This is a Request for Validation, whether it is done Consciously, or Unconsciously.
          • If the person you Shared your Excitement with Matches The Energy - you will Feel Connected, Understood, and Validated, like your Excitement is Justified and Deserve to Feel Good about it.
            • "Wow, that does suck!"
            • "Uh, yeah, I get that, long Conversations like that sure are tiring. Especially if the person is being so Frustratingly Stubborn..."
            • "That's so Exciting!"
              • "Right!? Thank you!!"
          • If the person you Shared your Excitement with does not Match Your Energy - you will Feel Disconnect - your Emotional Energy will drop and you will likely use further Emotional Energy to Defend/Justify/Analyze your Feeling Good, which is already Spoiling your Feeling Good, and you may even start to Doubt yourself if it's okay to Feel your Excitement in the first place... Especially if the person had Malice in their Expression, not just being Neutral.
    • Be Honest
      • When someone asks for your Opinion - it's Obvious and Feels infinitely worse to Sugarcoat the Truth, rather than Sharing your Honest Opinion.
        • Imagine playing a competitive game that means a lot to you, that you Prepared for by Practice for the past few weeks/months/years, and Under-Performing on the day of the Event.
          • Let's say this was the Rythm game Osu! and you Failed Miserably and Lost the competition.
          • Now Imagine you approach your Best Friend, and Express "I just don't know what happened with me; I messed up so bad! I Feel so Embarrassed!.."
            • And now Imagine if your Best Friend replied with:
              • "No you didn't mess up, you did great!"
                • You know that's not True. You Lost because you messed up. It was a Literal Skill Check, and you Lost.
                • This Expression is Disconnecting because you're not on the same page with your Best Friend, and Invalidating because you Feel like you Failed, and you Feel Embarrassed. Your Request for Validation has been Declined with Sugarcoating.
              • "Yeah, you did mess up. I don't know what happened with you today either. I saw you Practicing myself and today was so unlike you. How could you not Feel Frustrated..?"
                • You already knew you messed up, and Receiving Confirmation of your Observations and Validation of your Feelings is Connecting.
